Since the year 316 of the Laurentian Calendar, the Fishpeeker family has regularly assumed the role of Supermayor for the lake-covered Laurence country. Their triumph over the demonic Crabs that once terrorized the people of these lands would have been enough to make them royalty, but it was their devotion to kinship and cooperation that allowed their young nation to prosper as it has for the past 151 years. By sharing their methods of hunting the Crabs and harvesting their shells with their fellow Withered Peoples, lifespans that once reached a meager 45 years have doubled. Within a generation, the Fishpeekers were able to create the first unified nation-state in over 2,000 years.

In its current state, the Fishpeeker Mayoralty has dominated all of the rivers that flow to the great lakes. Each village in this sphere participates in an open flow of ideas and goods, usually traveling along the lakeside from region to region in a manner reflecting the traditional Laurentian Run. Beyond that, they have expanded as far as their current inertia has allowed. To the north, their will extents to the arctic desolation. East, to the peoples of the oceanic coast with whom they occasionally trade. West, to the border with the tribalistic Dakotans along Lady Sipper's River. And South, to the periphery that borders the mysterious mountain lands of the Ancients.

Though the peoples of greater Laurentia practice different religions, they and many others on the continent believe in the Lineage of the Rooster. It is said that once every few generations, a single person will be chosen by their god to lead the nation into prosperity. The Rooster can appear anywhere, so the actual identity of the current mantle-holder is often contested. In places where there is no definitive sighting of the chosen one, victory in battle is erroneously attributed to the spirit of The Rooster.
